Sprint Sunday 9am-5pm UIC Student Center East 605

Updated: Monday, March 23, 2015 - 12:42pm

We will be sprinting at UIC from 9 am - 5 pm and contributors of all skill sets and levels are welcome and encouraged. We will have a first-time sprinter workshop 9:30-10:30 to help new contributors get started and work efficiently. Please get a ticket (it is free) for Sunday so we know how many people will be attending. Lunch will be on your own.

Friday Night Social

Updated: Saturday, March 21, 2015 - 9:24am

The main room of the venue will be open in the evening for after-dinner sprinting and socializing, as an alternative to restaurants or crowding into a hotel room. Feel free to bring board games or other indoor activities.

Some dinner ideas are in the works, stay tuned!

See Social for more details.

Saturday Evening social

Updated: Monday, March 23, 2015 - 12:43pm

We'll be hanging out at Moxee Restaurant/Brewery on 724 W Maxwell St from 6-8PM on Saturday, March 21. We'll have drink tickets and lots of tasty food (BBQ and meat/cheese/gluten-free options) thanks to Acquia and Mailchimp, and there'll be a live Cajun-French band going on later. Come for the company, and stick around for some great tunes.

Be sure to pick up drink tickets at Acquia's booth in the keynote/lunch room.

Harrison St Bridge Construction

Updated: Tuesday, March 17, 2015 - 3:15pm

Due to the Jane Byrne Interchange construction, the Harrison St bridge between the Holiday Inn and UIC is closed. If you plan on walking to the venue, you'll have to take a detour either north to the Van Buren bridge or south to the Taylor St bridge. Luckily, the weather is looking to be pretty great (for March in Chicago) so you can enjoy a little more fresh air.

Volunteers Needed!

Updated: Monday, March 23, 2015 - 12:43pm

Volunteers are awesome and a big part of the conference’s success!  Please help us by signing up one or more shifts during MidCamp using the live Google spreadsheet. If you sign up, please also fill out this form so we can be in touch if we need you.

Room Monitor shifts are broken out to individual session times, please take a look and see if there is a session you plan on attending that might need a room monitor, you are encouraged to sign up for multiple shifts.

Thank you for your help and assistance filling these critical needs.

Speaker diversity is important to us

Updated: Friday, November 21, 2014 - 4:29pm

We believe a blind session selection process will help bring new and diverse speakers into the Drupal community.

Ashe Dryden's "Increasing Diversity at Your Conference" suggests having as diverse a session selection team as possible. You can help by volunteering to be part of that team. Tweet @midwestcamp to help.
